MIT's Random Hall

Random Hall is the smallest dorm on the not-so-far-off north side of the MIT campus. We are situated on a lovely section of Massachusetts Avenue just south of Central Square. We are conveniently located right next to a bar--- I mean, we are close to the Central T stop. : ) There are about 90 of us, divided into eight different floors, each with their own separate personalities. (Yes, we know the building's only four stories high. : )

Random Hall is a co-ed dorm, with four all-male floors, two all-female floors, and two co-ed floors. Each floor is equipped with its own kitchen, including refrigerators, stoves, sinks, appliances, and lots of cupboard space. A lot of people here choose to cook their own food instead of suffering from Aramark. Each floor also has a lounge where much leisure or tooling occurs.

Well, to tell you more about Random (this spiel is usually dragged out about the time Rush rolls around), we have a couple key features. We're one of the few dorms with a legally occupiable roofdeck. We also have the cheapest laundry on campus, featuring dorm-owned washers and dryers.

Our basement offers other forms of entertainment as well. We have a pool table, ping pong table, air hockey table, and foosball. There is also a piano room (that actually has a piano), a computer room (with our spiffy printer), and a weight room. We also have a beautiful new 27" color TV for our main lounge.

The constitution of Random Hall's house government is available.
