About Random Hall:
Random Hall is MIT's smallest dormitory, providing housing for 93 residents. We're located just north of campus at 290 Massachusetts Avenue in the great city of Cambridge, MA. If you're coming to MIT or Random, you can see our location on MIT's Campus Map in a zoomed-in or zoomed-out version. (To MIT people, we're also known as Building NW61). If you're coming from campus, this map may be helpful.

Random Hall opened in 1969, and to this date is still the only dorm on the northern side of the MIT campus. Our location near Central Square makes shopping and traveling through the entire Boston area extremely convenient. The MBTA Red Line is just a few blocks away, and a new grocery store and hotel are being constructed right behind the dorm during the Summer of 1997.

The dormitory is divided into eight "floors," each taking up half of a physical floor of the dorm. The floors, which house 14 residents, also have a complete kitchen with stove, microwave, and four refrigerators, two private bathrooms, and a lounge. Two of the floors are co-ed, two are all female, and the other four are designated as all-male floors.

In the basement of Random Hall is a computer room, the dormitory's MITnet printer, and a game room with ping pong and pool tables. Our three washers and four dryers, each costing just 25 cents, are also located in the basement; their status can be checked online . The first floor features a large lounge with daily subscriptions to the Boston Globe and The New York Times as well as a new 25-inch Sony stereo television. Our desk sorts mail and rents VCRs as well as movies from our extensive collection.

If you're coming to MIT in the fall, please check out our rush pages. If you have any questions about Random Hall, feel free to e-mail us.
