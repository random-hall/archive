5 Ways to See our Machines!
Random Hall's laundry server provides up-to-the-second information about our laundry machines over the internet. Query the machines via any of the methods below:
1
Our Java interface is the most interesting, as well as the most informative. It shows the status of the LED lights on the washers and driers as well as the time on (or off) and a bar graph of the time progression.
2
Our CGI interface is less informative, but useful for text-mode and non-Java enhanced browsers.
3
Finger the laundry server at laundry@laundry.mit.edu
4
Use the text mode Athena client:
add lisiecki; ttylaundry
(Use ttylaundry -t for live updates)
5
Use Zephyr notification:
zctl sub laundry \* \*
Random Hall laundry was developed by Phil Lisiecki and Kevin Lynch and is currently hosted by Jacob Schwartz . Please send questions and comments on the laundry server to random-laundry@mit.edu .

Soon, we'll also have a comments/repair request form for the laundry machines online.
